Nutrients Roles Food
Vitamin B B vitamins are involved in many roles, such as production energy and proper functioning of the nervous system. Most vitamins B are not stored by the body in sufficient quantities. Adequate intake vitamin B is important to ensure optimal energy production.
  • Green leafy vegetables
  • Eggs
  • Legumes
  • Milk
Vitamin C The concentration of vitamin C is particularly important in cells that protect the body’s immune system. It increases the mobility of white blood cells and stimulates the production of cytokines, the messengers activating the immune response while increasing the absorption of iron.
  • Broccoli
  • Pepper
  • Orange
  • Guava
Iron Iron plays a major role as a component of hemoglobin, a red blood cell protein responsible for transporting oxygen from the lungs to the body’s cells. It also intervenes in the process of cell division. Note that certain elements influence the assimilation of iron. Vitamin C will for example promote the absorption of iron. It is therefore interesting to eat vegetables with meat, a source of iron, that provide vitamin C.
  • Parsley
  • White beans
  • Quinoa
  • Chickpeas
